# Yantra Key — Visual Language of the Tattva Deck

Every card's image is a yantra — a geometric pattern generated from the card's own metadata. Nothing is decorative. Every shape, color, and composition choice encodes meaning from the tradition.

## The Base Shapes (from yantra tradition)

| Shape | Meaning | Tradition |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Bindu** (point/dot) | The seed. Undifferentiated consciousness. The source before any expansion. | Center of every yantra — what everything returns to |
| **Triangle pointing up** ▲ | Śiva/fire/will. Ascending energy, transformation, the impulse toward expansion (unmeṣa) | Agni (fire) in Vedic geometry |
| **Triangle pointing down** ▽ | Śakti/water/receptivity. Descending grace, creative power flowing into manifestation | Yoni symbol, the creative matrix |
| **Circle** ○ | Wholeness, cycle, the breath. No beginning, no ending. Air/knowing — perception touching everything equally | The boundary between inner and outer |
| **Square** □ | Earth, stability, ground, manifestation. The most contracted form — consciousness appearing as solidity | The bhūpura (earth fortress) of traditional yantras, always the outermost boundary |
| **Lotus petals** | Opening, receptivity, beauty emerging from mud. The number of petals varies by level | Padma — in chakra yantras, petals indicate the number of channels meeting at that center |
| **Rays** | Emanation, radiance, consciousness shining outward. More rays = more expansion | Prakāśa — the light of consciousness |
| **Concentric circles** | Pulsation, layers of experience, contraction toward center or expansion outward. Spanda made visible | Each ring is a level of the tattva system itself |

## Reading the Cards by Level

### L3: The Five Shaktis (simplest — one dominant shape)

These are the suits. Each is the purest expression of its shakti — minimal, powerful.

| Card | What You See | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Cit** (awareness) | Single bindu + 16 rays on deep blue | Pure awareness radiating. The bindu is consciousness. The rays are prakāśa — the light that illuminates everything. Blue = space, the element closest to pure awareness |
| **Ānanda** (bliss) | 8-petal lotus + bindu on dark | Bliss blooming. The lotus opens FROM mud — ānanda is not escape but the delight of consciousness recognizing itself in every experience. Silver = water's reflective quality |
| **Icchā** (will) | Single triangle up + bindu on red | Fire rising. The triangle points up because will is ascending energy — the impulse to create, to move, to become. Red = fire. The bindu at the apex is the point of focus |
| **Jñāna** (knowing) | Circle + 12 rays on green-gray | Knowing touches everything equally, like wind. The circle is wholeness. The rays are perception extending outward. Green-gray = air, invisible but felt |
| **Kriyā** (action) | Rotated square + bindu on gold | Action grounds itself. The square is earth — manifestation, solidity. Rotated 45° to suggest dynamic movement within stability. Gold = the ground beneath your feet |

### L2 Pure Tattvas: The Divine Descent

Each pure tattva shows consciousness at a different stage of self-expression. Watch how the pattern grows more complex as you descend:

| Card | What You See | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **T01 Shiva** | Bindu + 32 fine rays | The most expanded. Almost nothing — just the point of awareness and the subtlest possible radiance. 32 rays because the tradition associates Shiva with the fullest expression of emanation |
| **T02 Shakti** | 12-petal lotus + bindu | Vimarśa — consciousness reflecting on itself. The lotus is self-recognition blooming. 12 petals echo the 12 vowels of the Sanskrit alphabet — the sonic body of Shakti |
| **T03 Sadāshiva** | Triangle up + bindu on red | Will arising. "I am this" — but the "this" is still vague. The triangle is the first directional shape. Consciousness now has an impulse but not yet clarity |
| **T04 Īshvara** | Circle + inverted triangle | "This am I" — clarity. The circle is wholeness of knowing. The inverted triangle is Shakti's creative power now clearly perceived. The universe is visible but still recognized as self |
| **T05 Shuddha Vidyā** | Two interlocking triangles + white bindu | The threshold. Up-triangle (Shiva) and down-triangle (Shakti) perfectly balanced — echoing the Śrī Yantra. White bindu at center = pure awareness holding both. Last level where unity is felt |

### L2 Coverings: The Veiling

| Card | What You See | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **T06 Māyā** | Square inside circle + 5 horizontal lines | The square contains what the circle was. The horizontal lines are veils — consciousness covering itself. Purple = all shaktis being limited simultaneously |
| **T12 Purusha** | Same pattern, tighter | You. The limited self. Same veiling, more contracted |

### L2 Concepts: The Teachings Made Visible

| Card | What You See | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Trinity** (Shiva-Shakti-Nara) | Two interlocking triangles + white bindu | Star of David / hexagram pattern. Up = Shiva (transcendent). Down = Shakti (immanent). White center = Nara (you — where they meet). Three perspectives on one reality |
| **Spanda** | 8 concentric pulsing circles | The divine pulsation. Each ring is a wave of expansion and contraction. No center bindu because spanda is not a point — it is the movement between points. The pulse itself |
| **Pratyabhijñā** (recognition) | Two circles + bindu + 8 rays | The outer circle is what you think reality is. The inner circle is what it actually is. The bindu is the moment of recognition — when you see they were never separate. Rays = the light of recognition shining |
| **Svātantrya** (freedom) | 24 rays + circle + white bindu | Maximum radiation. Freedom shining in every direction. The circle contains but does not confine. White bindu = the unconditioned core that no covering can reach |
| **Three Malas** | 3 bindus in a triangle | Three impurities, three points of contraction. Āṇava (top left) = "I am small." Māyīya (top right) = "I am separate." Kārma (bottom) = "I am the doer." The triangle they form is not a yantra of power — it is the shape of forgetting |
| **Three Upāyas** | 3 concentric circles + white bindu | Three means of practice, nested. Outer = āṇavopāya (technique — you swim). Middle = śāktopāya (contemplation — you surf). Inner = śāmbhavopāya (grace — you are carried). White center = what all three point at |

### L1 Tattvas: The Full Compositions

Each L1 card combines elements based on its category:

| Category | Pattern | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| **Coverings** (kañcukas) | Square + concentric circles + bindu | Containment. The square contracts. The circles show layers of limitation. Each kañcuka has a slightly different size square — they're all the same structure at different scales |
| **Inner instrument** | Circle + triangle up + bindu | The psyche. The circle is the field of awareness. The triangle is discrimination rising. The bindu size increases from buddhi (small) through ahamkāra to manas (larger) — the ego expands |
| **Perception senses** | Circle + rays + lotus + bindu | Receiving. The circle is the open field. Rays = perception extending outward. Lotus = receptivity opening. More rays for higher-numbered senses (smell has the most — it is the oldest, most primal sense) |
| **Action senses** | Square + rays + bindu | Doing. The square grounds. The rays = action extending outward. More rays for higher-numbered senses |
| **Subtle elements** | Circle + concentric circles + bindu | Delicate. Potential not yet manifest. More rings for denser elements |
| **Gross elements** | Unique per element — see below | Each element gets its own composition |

### The Five Gross Elements: Full Yantras

| Card | What You See | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **T32 Space** (ākāsha) | Two circles, vast spacing, tiny bindu | Almost empty. The tiniest point in the largest space. This is what space IS — the maximum openness with the minimum form. Deep blue |
| **T33 Air** (vāyu) | Two circles + 8 inner rays + bindu | More active than space. The rays are wind — invisible movement. Still mostly open. Green-gray |
| **T34 Fire** (tejas) | Two nested triangles up + white bindu | Fire ascending. Two triangles = intensity doubled. White bindu = the light at the tip of the flame. Red. The species got fire before it had the grammars to hold it |
| **T35 Water** (jala) | Circle + 8-petal lotus + inner circle + bindu | Flow within containment. The lotus floats on water — beauty emerging from depth. Intimate, nourishing. Silver |
| **T36 Earth** (prithvī) | Three nested squares + bindu | Maximum contraction. Three layers of solidity — the most manifest, most grounded, most here. Gold. The axiom beneath the ground. Maybe it is earth talking |

## The Hidden Grammar

There is one more pattern you'll notice if you lay all 52 yantras out in order:

**From Shiva (T01) to Earth (T36), the images get denser.** More lines, more shapes, more containment. This IS the tattva descent — consciousness progressively contracting from a single bindu into nested squares of solid ground.

And if you reverse the order — from Earth back to Shiva — the images open. The squares dissolve into circles. The circles dissolve into rays. The rays dissolve into a single point. The point dissolves into the blue field.

That is the path of recognition. The construction becoming visible to itself.
