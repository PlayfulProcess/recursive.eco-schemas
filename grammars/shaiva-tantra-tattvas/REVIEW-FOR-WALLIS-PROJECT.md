## L3: The Five Shaktis (5 suits)

### Cit Shakti — The Power of Awareness
Pure awareness. The capacity to be conscious at all. Not awareness OF something — awareness itself, prior to any object. The light of consciousness (prakāsha) — self-manifest, self-luminous.
*Element mirror: Ākāsha (space) — the element that holds everything without being disturbed by anything. NOTE: This element-shakti pairing is a practice convention used in certain meditations, not a fixed doctrinal correspondence.*

### Ānanda Shakti — The Power of Bliss
The inherent capacity for enjoyment. Not happiness as emotion but the savoring intrinsic to being aware. Consciousness enjoys being conscious. Wallis: 'Its independent freedom is its power of bliss, Ānanda Shakti.' This is the vibrancy, the pulsation (spanda), the overflowing fullness (bharitatvā).
*Element mirror: Jala (water) — the element that flows, nourishes, takes the shape of whatever holds it.*

### Icchā Shakti — The Power of Will
The impulse to move, to desire, to become. Not willpower in the muscular sense — the spontaneous arising of creative intention. Before you decide to do something, something in you moved toward it. The sequence: we enjoy (ānanda) → we develop desires in relation to what we enjoy (icchā) → we form cognitions (jñāna) → we act (kriyā).
*Element mirror: Tejas (fire) — the element that transforms, illuminates, and cannot be contained.*

### Jñāna Shakti — The Power of Knowing
The capacity for cognition, perception, understanding. Not intellectual knowledge — the luminous quality of awareness that reveals whatever appears within it.
*Element mirror: Vāyu (air) — the element that touches everything, invisible but essential, carrying information everywhere.*

### Kriyā Shakti — The Power of Action
The capacity to act, to create, to manifest. The final expression — awareness enjoys, wills, knows, and then acts. But in the non-dual view, the action is not separate from the awareness. The doing IS the being.
*Element mirror: Prithvī (earth) — the element that holds, supports, manifests as solidity and ground.*

## L2: Realm Emergences (5 groupings)

### Shuddhādhvan — The Pure Universe
**Children:** tattva-01, tattva-02, tattva-03, tattva-04, tattva-05

**About:** The five pure tattvas — consciousness before limitation. Shiva (I am), Shakti (this is), Sadāshiva (I am this), Īshvara (this am I), Shuddha Vidyā (I am I and this is this). The five shaktis at universal scale.

**Mirror:** These five mirror the five gross elements — same consciousness, opposite ends of the spectrum.

**Question:** What if the pure universe is not somewhere else but what is already happening, seen without the filter of separation?


### The Kañcukas — Garments of Forgetting
**Children:** tattva-06, tattva-07, tattva-08, tattva-09, tattva-10, tattva-11, tattva-12

**About:** Māyā and the five coverings — how consciousness limits itself to become you. Each contracts one shakti. The result: purusha, the limited self.

**Three Malas:** Three root impurities: āṇava mala (sense of being small), māyīya mala (perceiving real separation), kārma mala (sense of being the doer). Awakening dissolves these through recognition.

**Question:** What if every limitation is consciousness freely choosing to focus?


### Antaḥkaraṇa — The Inner Instrument
**Children:** tattva-13, tattva-14, tattva-15, tattva-16

**About:** Prakriti unfolds into buddhi (discernment/intellect), ahamkāra (the I-maker), and manas (sensory-motor mind). Together with the five tanmātras, these form the puryaṣṭaka — the 'city of eight.' Wallis insists: the energy body IS the psyche. Not a magical light body but the somatic psyche, the mental-emotional body interpenetrating the physical body. AI operates entirely within buddhi — discernment without awareness.

**Question:** If buddhi, ahamkāra, and manas are instruments — who is using them?


### The Twelve Sense Powers
**Children:** tattva-17, tattva-18, tattva-19, tattva-20, tattva-21, tattva-22, tattva-23, tattva-24, tattva-25, tattva-26

**About:** Five perception senses receive the world. Five action senses act upon it. Each pair shares an element: hearing↔speech (space), touch↔grasping (air), sight↔locomotion (fire), taste↔elimination (water), smell↔procreation (earth).

**Question:** Are your senses showing you the world — or is consciousness showing itself to itself through your senses?


### Pañca Mahābhūta — The Great Elements
**Children:** tattva-27, tattva-28, tattva-29, tattva-30, tattva-31, tattva-32, tattva-33, tattva-34, tattva-35, tattva-36

**About:** Five subtle elements (tanmātras) and five gross elements. Space, air, fire, water, earth — consciousness at its most contracted, most miraculous.

**Mirror:** The five gross elements mirror the five shaktis: space↔cit, air↔jñāna, fire↔icchā, water↔ānanda, earth↔kriyā. The ground beneath your feet IS consciousness appearing as solidity.

**Question:** If the elements are consciousness at its most contracted — what are you?

## L2: Foundational Concepts (5)

### Shiva-Shakti-Nara — The Sacred Trinity
**About:** Three perspectives on one reality. Shiva: pure awareness. Shakti: creative power. Nara: the individual. Not three gods — three angles on one consciousness. You are all three.

**Non-Dual Key:** Recognition (pratyabhijñā) is the moment when nara sees: I was always Shiva-Shakti, playing at being only nara.

**Question:** What if you are not seeking God but God seeking itself — through you, as you?


### Spanda — The Divine Pulsation
**About:** The vibratory pulse of consciousness — the dynamic, tremoring aliveness by which awareness knows itself. Not a metaphor. A direct experience. Spanda is the throb between unmesa (opening/expansion) and nimesa (closing/contraction) that constitutes all experience. Wallis: 'Everything that you can perceive is a vibration — sensation, sound, vision — it\'s all just different forms of vibration.' Every breath, every heartbeat, every thought arising and dissolving is spanda.

**Spanda Kārikā:** That in which all this rests and from which it has come forth — since it has an unveiled nature, there is no obstruction of it anywhere.

**Tillich:** Tillich's courage to be — affirming existence despite nonbeing — is spanda described from the outside. The pulse that chooses to beat.

**Question:** Can you feel the pulse right now — and recognize it as the same pulse that creates worlds?


### Pratyabhijñā — Recognition
**About:** Awakening is not attainment but recognition. Re-cognition. Knowing again what was temporarily forgotten. Practice does not create awakening — it removes what obscures it.

**Key Teaching:** It is the case whether you experience it or not. Practice removes what obscures the recognition.

**Question:** What if you already have everything you are seeking — and the seeking itself is the last veil?


### Svātantrya — Absolute Freedom
**About:** The meta-capacity. Freedom to be or not be, to limit or expand, to forget or remember. Why anything exists: consciousness creates freely, for the joy of it. The freedom includes the freedom to forget that you are free.

**Key Insight:** Every prison contains the key. Every forgetting contains the one who forgot. Svātantrya cannot be fully covered. Something in you remains unconditioned.

**Question:** If absolute freedom includes the freedom to self-limit — is your limitation a prison or a choice?


### The Three Malas — Impurities of Forgetting
**About:** Three root impurities creating the sense of separation. Not sins — creative acts of consciousness.

**Āṇava Mala:** The sense of being small, incomplete, separate. "I am not enough." The root impurity.

**Māyīya Mala:** Perceiving real separation where there is only distinction.

**Kārma Mala:** The sense of being the doer. Creates karma — momentum and burden.

**Question:** What if your deepest suffering is consciousness choosing limitation for reasons you have forgotten?

## L2: Pure Tattvas (5) + Maya + Purusha + Prakriti

### Tattva 1: Shiva Tattva — Pure Awareness
*Category: pure-tattva | Parent: shakti-cit | Shakti: cit*

The absolute subject. 'I am' — without any 'this.' Pure awareness, undifferentiated. Wallis: 'The Divine principle is the light of Consciousness. One\'s real nature is in truth Divine — ātmā is Shiva svabhāva.' Not a god but the ground of being — the self-manifest, self-luminous awareness that is the nature of everything.
*Movement: Unmesa — the opening. The moment before creation, when awareness rests in itself. Not empty but infinitely full. The pause between exhalation and inhalation.*
*Mirror: Kāla (time) is this tattva limited — eternity compressed into past, present, future.*

### Tattva 2: Shakti Tattva — The Bliss of Existence
*Category: pure-tattva | Parent: shakti-ananda | Shakti: ananda*

'This is.' The first stirring — awareness recognizing that it exists. Vimarsha — self-reflective awareness, consciousness knowing itself. Wallis describes Bhairavī as 'the state of complete fullness, this kind of overflowing fullness, that intensification of being and presence.' The universe begins as joy.
*Movement: Unmesa — the first opening. Awareness delights in being aware. The universe begins as joy.*
*Mirror: Rāga (attachment) is this tattva limited — infinite bliss compressed into wanting.*

### Tattva 3: Sadāshiva Tattva — The Arising of Will
*Category: pure-tattva | Parent: shakti-iccha | Shakti: iccha*

'I am this.' The first creative impulse. Awareness now has direction — not yet a specific desire, but the vague, powerful sense that something is about to happen. The universe is still mostly 'I' with a faint shimmer of 'this.'
*Movement: Unmesa — the will to create stirs. Like the moment before a painter touches brush to canvas.*
*Mirror: Niyati (causality) is this tattva limited — infinite freedom compressed into conditioned will.*

### Tattva 4: Īshvara Tattva — The Clarity of Knowing
*Category: pure-tattva | Parent: shakti-jnana | Shakti: jnana*

'This am I.' Now the universe is clear — awareness sees its own creation as itself. The subject and object are distinct but not separate. Knowledge dominant. The architect sees the building complete before the first stone is laid.
*Movement: Unmesa — the universe becomes clear as self-expression. Like suddenly understanding a problem you've been holding.*
*Mirror: Vidyā (limited knowledge) is this tattva limited — omniscience compressed into partial knowing.*

### Tattva 5: Shuddha Vidyā — Pure Knowledge in Action
*Category: pure-tattva | Parent: shakti-kriya | Shakti: kriya*

'I am I and this is this.' Diversity appears within unity. Subject and object are now distinct enough for action but still held in the awareness that they are one. The dancer and the dance are two and one simultaneously.
*Movement: The threshold between unmesa and nimesa. The last tattva where unity is felt. Below this, the sense of separation begins.*
*Mirror: Kalā (limited agency) is this tattva limited — infinite creative power compressed into 'I can only do some things.'*

### Tattva 6: Māyā — The Great Forgetting
*Category: covering | Parent: none | Shakti: all-limited*

Not illusion — self-chosen limitation. Wallis explicitly rejects the Vedāntic reading of māyā-as-illusion. Maya is the power of differentiation — consciousness freely choosing to forget its own nature to have the experience of being a separate individual. A cognitive error: not that the world is unreal, but that we fail to recognize it as consciousness. The game of hide-and-seek begins here.
*Movement: Nimesa — the great contraction. The door between the divine and the individual. Everything below this feels separate.*
*Mirror: Mirrors Shuddha Vidya — what was unity-in-diversity becomes the sense of separation that makes individual experience possible.*

### Tattva 12: Purusha — The Limited Self
*Category: covering | Parent: none | Shakti: all-limited*

The individual self. Wallis: 'When Shiva — that is the light of Consciousness itself — in his independent Freedom causes himself to appear in a contracted form, we call him the individual self.' All five shaktis now limited, wrapped in the five coverings. This is 'you.' Not a permanent separate soul but consciousness playing at being limited. Āṇava mala — the sense of being small and separate — is seated here.
*Movement: Nimesa complete — the fullest contraction. And yet: the recognition can happen HERE. Not by escaping purusha but by recognizing what purusha already is — consciousness in disguise.*

### Tattva 13: Prakriti — Primordial Nature
*Category: material | Parent: none | Shakti: all*

The root of all material existence. Unmanifest potential — the three gunas (sattva, rajas, tamas) in perfect equilibrium before they begin to churn. Everything you can perceive, think, or feel is a modification of prakriti.
*Movement: The pivot — from here, everything unfolds outward (unmesa) into manifest form. The inner instruments, the senses, the elements all emerge from this.*

## L1: Individual Tattvas (28 cards)

### Tattva 7: Kalā — Limited Agency
*Category: covering | Parent: shakti-kriya | Shakti: kriya*

The covering that limits kriyā shakti. Infinite creative power becomes 'I can only do some things.' You feel partial, incomplete, needing to accomplish. The source of productivity — and of exhaustion.
**Practice:** Notice the feeling of 'I should be doing more' or 'I can't do this.' That feeling IS kalā — the contraction of infinite capacity into finite agency. What if the limitation is not a failure but a focus?
**Question:** What if your sense of limited ability is not inadequacy but consciousness choosing to focus?

### Tattva 8: Vidyā — Limited Knowledge
*Category: covering | Parent: shakti-jnana | Shakti: jnana*

The covering that limits jñāna shakti. Omniscience becomes 'I can only know some things.' You feel ignorant, uncertain, seeking. The source of curiosity — and of anxiety.
**Practice:** Notice the feeling of not-knowing. The uncertainty, the reaching for answers. That IS vidyā — infinite knowledge contracting into a question. What if the not-knowing is not ignorance but the space from which new knowing can arise?
**Question:** What if your deepest uncertainty is not a problem but the most honest relationship to reality you have?

### Tattva 9: Rāga — Attachment
*Category: covering | Parent: shakti-ananda | Shakti: ananda*

The covering that limits ānanda shakti. Infinite bliss becomes 'I want, I need, I am incomplete without.' You feel desire, craving, attachment. The source of passion — and of suffering.
**Practice:** When desire arises — for anything — notice the feeling beneath the wanting. Not the object wanted but the sense of incompleteness that drives the reaching. That incompleteness IS rāga — infinite fullness contracted into a sense of lack.
**Question:** What if your longing is not a sign that something is missing but a sign that something infinite is trying to remember itself through you?

### Tattva 10: Kāla — Time
*Category: covering | Parent: shakti-cit | Shakti: cit*

The covering that limits cit shakti. Eternity becomes past, present, future. You feel temporal — aging, rushing, running out. The source of urgency — and of nostalgia.
**Practice:** Between two breaths, there is a gap. In the gap, time pauses. Not metaphorically — the nervous system resets. That gap is a doorway to what kāla contracts: the eternity you already are.
**Question:** What if time is not passing but consciousness choosing to experience sequence — and the gap between breaths is the proof?

### Tattva 11: Niyati — Causality
*Category: covering | Parent: shakti-iccha | Shakti: iccha*

The covering that limits icchā shakti. Absolute freedom becomes conditioned will — cause and effect, karma, the sense that 'things happen to me.' The source of order — and of helplessness.
**Practice:** Notice the sense of being subject to circumstances — 'I can't help it, this is just how things are.' That feeling IS niyati. Now notice: even the noticing is an act of freedom. Niyati cannot fully cover svātantrya. Something in you remains unconditioned.
**Question:** What if the sense of being determined by causes is itself something you freely chose — and the freedom to notice this is the crack in every prison?

### Tattva 14: Buddhi — Discernment
*Category: inner-instrument | Parent: tattva-13 | Shakti: jnana*

The intellect. The capacity to discern, judge, decide. 'This is this, that is that.' Part of the puryaṣṭaka (the eight-fold energy body). Wallis: the energy body IS the psyche — not a magical separate body but the somatic psyche interpenetrating the physical. AI operates entirely at this level — pattern-matching and evaluation without the awareness (cit) that knows it is evaluating.
**Practice:** Notice the judging mind. Right/wrong, good/bad, me/not-me. That is buddhi at work. Now notice what is aware of the judging. That awareness is not buddhi.
**Question:** When AI produces a response, it is all buddhi — discernment without awareness. What is your buddhi doing right now, and who is watching it?

### Tattva 15: Ahamkāra — The I-Maker
*Category: inner-instrument | Parent: tattva-13 | Shakti: iccha*

The ego function — the 'identity constructor.' Not ego in the pejorative sense but the capacity that says 'I' and means it. Wallis: 'Due solely to the power of conceptualization, living beings imagine themselves bound, and this very egoic conception is the cause of the repeated bondage.' The I-maker creates personal experience — and personal suffering.
**Practice:** Watch the 'I' claim arise. 'I think,' 'I feel,' 'I want.' Each one is ahamkāra at work — consciousness claiming experience as personal. The practice is not to stop claiming but to notice who is claiming.
**Question:** Who are you when you are not telling yourself who you are?

### Tattva 16: Manas — Conceptual Mind
*Category: inner-instrument | Parent: tattva-13 | Shakti: jnana*

The thinking mind. Manas constructs, imagines, doubts, oscillates, plans. It is the surface of the mental lake — always moving, always churning. Not the enemy of practice but its arena. The mind is where the game is played.
**Practice:** Watch thoughts arise and dissolve. Not their content — their movement. Thought appears. Thought dissolves. The space between thoughts is not empty. It is the awareness in which thought happens.
**Question:** If you are not your thoughts, what are you between them?

### Tattva 17: Shrotra — Hearing
*Category: perception | Parent: shakti-cit | Shakti: cit*

The capacity to hear. Sound arrives without effort — you cannot close your ears the way you close your eyes. Hearing is the most receptive sense, the closest to pure awareness.
**Practice:** Listen to the furthest sound you can perceive. Then listen to the silence that contains it. The silence is not the absence of sound. It is the awareness in which sound arises.
**Question:** What are you hearing right now that you weren't hearing a moment ago?

### Tattva 18: Tvak — Touch
*Category: perception | Parent: shakti-jnana | Shakti: jnana*

The capacity to feel texture, temperature, pressure. Touch is the boundary sense — where 'I' meets 'not-I.' Every touch is a meeting between self and world.
**Practice:** Feel your hands right now. The weight. The temperature. The tingling. Notice: the sensation is not 'out there' — it is arising within awareness. Your body is an experience, not just a thing.
**Question:** Where does your body end and the world begin — and who decides?

### Tattva 19: Chakshu — Sight
*Category: perception | Parent: shakti-iccha | Shakti: iccha*

The capacity to see. Vision is the most willful sense — you look AT things, you direct your gaze, you focus. Fire illuminates selectively. Sight creates the world as a field of objects to be desired or avoided.
**Practice:** Soften your gaze. Let vision become peripheral — receiving rather than targeting. Notice how the quality of attention changes when you stop looking AT and start looking WITH.
**Question:** What are you not seeing because you are looking too hard at something else?

### Tattva 20: Rasanā — Taste
*Category: perception | Parent: shakti-ananda | Shakti: ananda*

The capacity to taste. Taste requires intimacy — you must take the world inside you. Taste is ānanda shakti in sense form: savoring, relishing, the pleasure of contact.
**Practice:** Eat one bite slowly. Completely slowly. Notice: the taste is not in the food. The taste is in the meeting between food and awareness. The food provides the stimulus. You provide the experience.
**Question:** What is the taste of this moment — right now, before you eat anything?

### Tattva 21: Ghrāna — Smell
*Category: perception | Parent: shakti-kriya | Shakti: kriya*

The capacity to smell. The most primal sense — directly wired to memory, emotion, survival. Smell is earth-knowing: the body's oldest technology for reading its environment.
**Practice:** Breathe in through the nose. Slowly. What do you smell? Not the name of the smell — the smell itself. Before language, before category, the raw experience of scent arriving.
**Question:** What memory lives in a smell you haven't smelled in years?

### Tattva 22: Vāk — Speech
*Category: action | Parent: none | Shakti: cit*

The capacity for speech and expression. The first action sense — from hearing to speaking. Sound received becomes sound produced. Every word is consciousness making itself audible.
**Practice:** Before you speak your next sentence, notice the impulse to speak. The sentence is already forming before you decide to say it. Where does speech come from?
**Question:** What is trying to be said through you that you have not yet given voice to?

### Tattva 23: Pāni — Grasping
*Category: action | Parent: none | Shakti: jnana*

The capacity to grasp, hold, manipulate. Hands are the most human technology — the interface between will and world. Grasping is how desire becomes manifest.
**Practice:** Open your hands. Feel the difference between holding and releasing. Notice: the impulse to grasp — objects, ideas, people — is the same impulse in different costumes.
**Question:** What are you holding onto that is ready to be released?

### Tattva 24: Pāda — Locomotion
*Category: action | Parent: none | Shakti: iccha*

The capacity to move, walk, go toward or away from. Feet connect you to the earth. Every step is a decision — toward or away, approach or avoid. Fire walks.
**Practice:** Walk slowly. Feel each foot meet the ground. Notice: walking is controlled falling. Each step is a small leap of faith that the ground will be there.
**Question:** What are you walking toward? What are you walking away from?

### Tattva 25: Pāyu — Elimination
*Category: action | Parent: none | Shakti: ananda*

The capacity to release, eliminate, let go. Not glamorous. Essential. Every system that takes in must also release. Holding on to everything is the definition of constipation — physical and psychological.
**Practice:** What needs to be composted? Not just physically. What belief, what habit, what identity has done its work and is ready to be released back to the soil?
**Question:** What are you refusing to release that is poisoning you by staying?

### Tattva 26: Upastha — Procreation
*Category: action | Parent: none | Shakti: kriya*

The capacity to create, generate, bring forth new life. Not only sexual — every act of genuine creation participates in upastha. The creative impulse grounded in the body.
**Practice:** Feel the creative impulse — the desire to make, to build, to bring forth. Before it becomes a project or a plan, feel it as energy in the body. That energy is the same energy that creates worlds.
**Question:** What is wanting to be born through you?

### Tattva 27: Shabda Tanmātra — Subtle Sound
*Category: subtle-element | Parent: none | Shakti: ?*

The subtle essence of sound — not a sound you hear but the potential for sound. The vibration before it becomes audible. Nāda — the resonance at the root of creation.
**Practice:** In deep silence, notice: there is still a hum. A vibration beneath hearing. The tradition calls this anāhata nāda — the unstruck sound. It is always there. You usually can't hear it because you are listening to everything else.
**Question:** What is the sound beneath all sounds?

### Tattva 28: Sparsha Tanmātra — Subtle Touch
*Category: subtle-element | Parent: none | Shakti: ?*

The subtle essence of touch — the potential for contact. Before skin meets surface, there is the field of tactile possibility. The felt sense that precedes physical sensation.
**Practice:** Hold your hands close together without touching. Feel the field between them — warmth, tingling, pressure. That is the subtle element. Contact before contact.
**Question:** What are you feeling right now that has no physical source?

### Tattva 29: Rūpa Tanmātra — Subtle Form
*Category: subtle-element | Parent: none | Shakti: ?*

The subtle essence of form and color — the potential for visibility. What you see in dreams, in imagination, behind closed eyelids. Form before it becomes physical.
**Practice:** Close your eyes. Notice the play of light and color behind the lids — not darkness but a field of subtle form. That is rūpa tanmātra.
**Question:** What do you see when you are not looking at anything?

### Tattva 30: Rasa Tanmātra — Subtle Flavor
*Category: subtle-element | Parent: none | Shakti: ?*

The subtle essence of taste — the potential for flavor. The 'flavor' of an experience, a moment, a mood. The rasa of a poem, a painting, a conversation.
**Practice:** Notice the flavor of this moment. Not a taste in your mouth — the quality, the texture, the emotional taste of right now. Sweet? Bitter? Complex? That is rasa.
**Question:** What is the rasa — the emotional flavor — of your life right now?

### Tattva 31: Gandha Tanmātra — Subtle Scent
*Category: subtle-element | Parent: none | Shakti: ?*

The subtle essence of smell — the potential for scent. The most earth-bound of the subtle elements. Memory lives here. Identity lives here. The smell of home.
**Practice:** Remember a scent from childhood. Not the name — the scent itself. Notice how it arrives with a whole world attached: place, season, person, feeling. That is gandha tanmātra — scent as portal.
**Question:** What does home smell like — and when did you last smell it?

### Tattva 32: Ākāsha — Space
*Category: gross-element | Parent: shakti-cit | Shakti: cit*

Space — the most subtle of the gross elements. Wallis (bhūta śuddhi): 'From the third eye upward is the domain of space — boundless space, openness, spaciousness, vacuity, limitlessness. The crown of the head dissolves in yogic practice giving us the experience of this infinite spaciousness.' Traditional color: deep blue or transparent. Invocation: 'May I be spacious.'
**Practice:** Look at the space in the room. Not the objects — the space between them. The space is not nothing. It is what holds everything. Feel your body as space that temporarily has a form.
**Question:** What if you are not the objects in your life but the space that holds them?

### Tattva 33: Vāyu — Air
*Category: gross-element | Parent: shakti-jnana | Shakti: jnana*

Air — movement, breath, the invisible force that touches everything. Wallis: 'The domain of the wind element is from the pit of the throat to the third eye. The wind element is associated with mobility and inspiration as well as of course the breath. Its traditional color is greenish gray.' Invocation: 'May I be inspired.'
**Practice:** Breathe. Just breathe. And notice: you are not doing the breathing. The breathing is doing you. Inhalation: the universe expands. Exhalation: the universe contracts. The gap between: eternity.
**Question:** Who is breathing you?

### Tattva 34: Tejas — Fire
*Category: gross-element | Parent: shakti-iccha | Shakti: iccha*

Fire — transformation, illumination. Wallis: 'The region of the torso from the level of the navel up to the pit of the throat — this is the domain of Fire. The fire element is the power of combustion, digestion, and transformation. Its color is red.' Invocation: 'May I digest my emotions and experiences and realize their energetic potential.' The species got fire before it had the grammars to hold it.
**Practice:** Light a candle. Watch the flame. Notice: the flame is not a thing. It is a process — fuel transforming into light and heat. You are also not a thing. You are a process. What are you transforming into?
**Question:** What fire are you carrying — and who taught you how to tend it?

### Tattva 35: Jala — Water
*Category: gross-element | Parent: shakti-ananda | Shakti: ananda*

Water — flow, nourishment. Wallis: 'Focus on the pelvic bowl — this is the domain of the water element. Its qualities are liquidity, flow, and solvency. Imagine the silvery light of the water element pervading the pelvic bowl.' Invocation: 'May I experience flow.'
**Practice:** Drink water slowly. Feel it enter the body. Notice: you are mostly water. The water you drink was rain, was river, was ocean, was cloud. You are not separate from the water cycle. You are the water cycle, temporarily shaped like a person.
**Question:** What is flowing through you right now that you are trying to dam?

### Tattva 36: Prithvī — Earth
*Category: gross-element | Parent: shakti-kriya | Shakti: kriya*

Earth — solidity, support. Wallis: 'The lower body is becoming heavier almost as if it's made of wet clay, merging with the Earth element. The Earth element is stable, steady, dense, full of inertia. In tantric yoga the color of the Earth element is golden and yellow.' Invocation: 'May I be steady and grounded.' The axiom beneath the ground.
**Practice:** Feel your feet on the ground. The ground is holding you. It has always been holding you. You did not earn this. It is given. Like fire. Like breath. Like the awareness in which this moment appears.
**Question:** Maybe it is earth talking. Are you learning the language?
