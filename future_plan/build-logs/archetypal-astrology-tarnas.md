## Key Learnings

- **Tarnas's system is planet-centered, not sign-centered.** This is a crucial distinction from traditional astrology. The zodiac signs are NOT the primary organizing principle — the planetary archetypes are. Signs modify planets, but the planets carry the archetypal weight.
- **Planetary aspects are natural L2 emergence.** When two planets combine (conjunction, opposition, square), an emergent archetypal dynamic appears that is MORE than the sum of its parts. Saturn-Pluto is not just "Saturn + Pluto" — it's "The Hard Reckoning," a specific historical and psychological pattern.
- **Historical correlations are the evidence base.** Unlike traditional astrology's character descriptions, Tarnas's system is grounded in documented historical correlations. Each L2 aspect includes specific historical examples — this is what makes Cosmos and Psyche intellectually compelling.
- **The Prometheus insight is non-negotiable.** You cannot build a Tarnas grammar and assign Ouranos's archetype to Uranus. Tarnas's most original contribution is the argument that Uranus-Prometheus, not Uranus-Ouranos, explains the planet's observed correlations (rebellion, innovation, awakening — not sky-father conservatism).
- **Shadow sections are essential for astrology grammars.** Every archetype has a shadow expression. Without it, astrology becomes fortune-telling rather than depth psychology.
