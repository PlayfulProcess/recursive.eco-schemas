# Walt Whitman Grammar — Public-Domain Illustration Research

Research date: 2026-04-20. All images listed are works whose creators died before 1920 and/or were published in the U.S. before 1930, making them unambiguously public domain under current U.S. copyright law. Primary sources: Library of Congress Prints & Photographs Online Catalog (LoC P&P), the Walt Whitman Archive (WWA), Biodiversity Heritage Library (BHL), Wikimedia Commons, and Audubon.org.

LoC rights statement used throughout: *"No known restrictions on publication"* (the standard LoC declaration for pre-1929 U.S. material in their collection).

## Phase I — The 1855 Eruption

### Hollyer frontispiece engraving (MUST-HAVE cover candidate)
- **Title:** Walt Whitman, steel engraving for the frontispiece of *Leaves of Grass*, 1855
- **Creator:** Samuel Hollyer (1826–1919), engraved after Gabriel Harrison daguerreotype (July 1854)
- **PD status:** Hollyer d. 1919 (>70 yrs); published 1855 — unambiguous PD (U.S. and international)
- **LoC record:** Feinberg-Whitman Collection holds the engraving. Search: https://www.loc.gov/pictures/search/?q=hollyer+whitman
- **WWA page images of the 1855 frontispiece:** https://whitmanarchive.org/published/LG/1855/whole.html (click first plate)
- **Rights:** LoC — no known restrictions; WWA — open-access scholarly use
- **Wikimedia Commons alternate (high-res TIFF available):** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Walt_Whitman,_steel_engraving,_July_1854.jpg

### 1855 title page / first-edition cover
- **Title:** *Leaves of Grass*, Brooklyn, 1855 — title page and green cloth binding
- **Creator:** Whitman himself (designer); printed by Rome Brothers, Brooklyn
- **PD status:** Published 1855 — PD worldwide
- **Source:** WWA page-image edition — https://whitmanarchive.org/published/LG/1855/whole.html
- **Also held at LoC Rare Book Division and digitized via Internet Archive:** https://archive.org/details/leavesofgrass00whitrich

### 1855 manuscript leaves
- **Title:** Whitman notebooks and early manuscript fragments ("I am the poet...")
- **PD status:** pre-1892 manuscript pages — PD
- **Source:** LoC Thomas Biggs Harned Collection — https://www.loc.gov/collections/walt-whitman-papers/
- **WWA manuscript search:** https://whitmanarchive.org/manuscripts/

## Top Cover Candidates (ranked)

1. **Hollyer 1855 frontispiece engraving** — the iconic origin image; best for the grammar's cover. Matches the "eruption" energy. Wikimedia Commons hi-res available.
2. **Eakins 1887–88 oil portrait** (PAFA) — the definitive late-life face; rich color; art-historical weight.
3. **Cox 1887 "The Laughing Philosopher"** — Whitman's own favorite; warmth and humor.
4. **Brady ca. 1862 seated portrait** — mid-career bridge between the 1855 young man and the 1887 sage.
5. **Whitman with butterfly, Phillips & Taylor, 1873** (LC-DIG-ppmsca-07141, https://www.loc.gov/resource/ppmsca.07141) — whimsical, widely loved, unusual.

## Gaps and Recommendations

- **Harleigh Cemetery tomb:** no stable institutional PD photo found. Use Blake's "Death's Door" engraving as symbolic substitute, or a user-contributed Commons image (verify CC0/PD per-image), or a text-only card.
- **Brooklyn ferry 1850s:** no direct LoC engraving specifically of East River ferries in the 1850s surfaced in search. Use a Currier & Ives NY harbor lithograph from the period, or a Bachmann Brooklyn panorama (1846). Acceptable substitute: 1870s *Harper's Weekly* ferry engraving (visually close, unambiguously PD via Chronicling America).
- **Brooklyn printer (1830s):** no specific Whitman-printshop image exists. Use a generic 1850s-era wood engraving of a composing room or hand-press from LoC P&P as a period-accurate stand-in.
- **Specific manuscript of "Song of Myself":** the complete 1855 manuscript is lost (per WWA encyclopedia). Use the 1855 printed title page or surviving notebook fragments in LoC Harned Collection.
- For any card where no single image is clearly iconic, the safest fallback is: (a) a Whitman portrait from the matching decade, (b) a BHL botanical/zoological engraving thematically linked to the poem, or (c) a text-only card with a handwriting flourish.

## Notes on Due Diligence

- **LoC P&P:** always cite the `/item/` or `/resource/` permalink and the LC-DIG reproduction number. The "Rights Advisory" field on the item page is the authoritative statement.
- **Whitman Archive:** images are provided for scholarly use. The underlying originals (pre-1929 photographs of a subject who died 1892) are PD; WWA reproductions inherit that status.
- **Wikimedia Commons:** confirm the license tag per-file. Prefer files with `{{PD-US}}` or `{{PD-old-100}}`.
- **Biodiversity Heritage Library:** plates published pre-1929 are PD. BHL image download includes rights metadata.
- **Eakins painting:** PAFA states "no known copyright restrictions." 2D faithful reproductions of PD paintings are also PD in the U.S. per *Bridgeman v. Corel* (1999).
