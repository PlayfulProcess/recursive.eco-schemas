# The Body, the Child, and the Climate Week — Grammar Plan

*Status: placeholder plan. Content arc is being held for future development. Source material partly in the author's life, partly in Substack archive, partly in Gabrielle Roth / Ariès-correction / Josh-Schrei / Vera-Stanley-Alder research that is not yet done.*

## Summary

A grammar built around three intertwined threads the author has been carrying:

1. **The body doesn't lie** — somatic wisdom traditions (Gabrielle Roth's 5 Rhythms, Peter Levine, Bessel van der Kolk), the body as the reliable narrator we were trained to distrust.
2. **The multi-age child** — Ariès's 1960 claim that modernity invented childhood is largely discredited; what modernity invented was the age-segregated institution (compulsory schooling, age-graded peer groups). Indigenous multi-age play is the human default; Montessori / Waldorf / Reggio Emilia *recovered* rather than invented the approach.
3. **The climate-week scene** — a mother in NYC at climate week with her daughter, navigating the plastic-wrapped lunch problem, finding that the maps-of-plastic-free-eating approach is closer to hunter-gatherer provisioning than the kitchen-bound purity frame is.

These three threads converge on one argument: *the body knows what is adaptive; the institutions that alienated us from the body are modern; the recovery is available through practices that were always mainstream before they were given single-inventor names.*

## Why a grammar rather than only a chapter

The author is deciding separately whether this material belongs in the Axiom book. If it does (as Ch10g), the grammar here can serve as research / card-form companion. If it does not, the grammar stands alone as a recursive.eco deck or as the seed of a short companion volume.

## Proposed structure

### 18-24 cards across 3 clusters

**Cluster 1: The Body Doesn't Lie** (6-8 cards)
- Gabrielle Roth's 5 Rhythms — the five movement-states (Flowing / Staccato / Chaos / Lyrical / Stillness) as a somatic grammar
- Van der Kolk's *The Body Keeps the Score* — the body as record-keeper
- Peter Levine's *Waking the Tiger* — somatic experiencing
- Porges's polyvagal theory — how the body governs connection
- Linehan's Wise Mind — the body/emotion/intellect integration
- Tronick's still-face experiment — the infant body telling the truth the adult face is hiding

**Cluster 2: The Multi-Age Child** (6-8 cards)
- Ariès's claim and its subsequent dismantling (Shulamith Shahar, Linda Pollock, Nicholas Orme)
- The indigenous multi-age play default — examples from the author's own visits to indigenous communities
- Montessori's "method" as a recovery, not an invention — her debt to earlier pedagogies and to the observations of working mothers
- Waldorf / Steiner — the spiritual framing of what is also a recovery-of-the-body in education
- Reggio Emilia — the post-war Italian articulation of the same move
- The age-segregated classroom as the specific aberration of industrial modernity
- Dimitri Christakis's research on early-childhood screens and the body-dysregulation they produce
- Haidt's *The Anxious Generation* — the phone-child version of the same diagnosis

**Cluster 3: The Climate Week Scene and the Hunter-Gatherer Return** (4-6 cards)
- The plastic-wrapped lunch problem
- The map of plastic-free eating places (idea for the author to publish)
- Hunter-gatherer provisioning — foraging, food-carrying, communal provisioning across scales
- The kitchen as industrial innovation (Ruth Schwartz Cowan, *More Work for Mother*)
- Cildo Meireles's *Inserções em Circuitos Ideológicos* as a conceptual template for intervening in consumer systems
- The mother-child walking foraging/mapping scene as a Brazilian-indigenous move in New York

### Card schema

Same as other grammars — `expression` / `literal` / `about` / `scene` / `song_anchor` or similar. Body-tradition cards should have `practice_url` (link to a 5 Rhythms demo, a polyvagal exercise, etc.) where legal.

## Copyright

- **Gabrielle Roth** — died 2012; her books (*Maps to Ecstasy*, *Sweat Your Prayers*) still in copyright. Grammar cards will describe the five rhythms and their movement-states in the author's own words, not quote Roth.
- **Ariès** — died 1984; *Centuries of Childhood* still in copyright. Paraphrase only.
- **Van der Kolk, Levine, Porges** — all living; books in copyright. Reference work, paraphrase findings, quote ≤15 words with attribution.
- **Cildo Meireles** — born 1948; work documented in scholarly sources, cite exhibitions and secondary literature.
- **Indigenous knowledge** — CARE Principles apply. Generic references to multi-age child practice across cultures are fine; specific teachings from specific communities require permissions.
- **Photographic material** — author's own photos (e.g. from her visits to indigenous communities, if she has them and wants to use them) are safe. Otherwise use PD or commissioned.
