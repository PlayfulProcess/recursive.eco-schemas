# The Mythic Journey: Alice, Shiryo & Bishisheshi Visit the Wise Heart

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Status:** PLAN — awaiting approval

---

## The Big Idea

Alice (who has already fallen down one rabbit hole and walked through one looking-glass) finds a new door — and on the other side, she meets Shiryo (a small handmade robot learning to care for living things) and Bishisheshi (a kitten who IS a living thing). Together, they journey through Mythic Land — a place where every story ever told lives.

Each stop on the journey is a myth, a folktale, or a movie moment. At each stop, one of the three characters encounters a DBT skill — not as a lesson, but as an experience they live through. They watch, they feel, they try. And then they carry what they learned back into their own lives.

**The frame**: Alice asks questions (she's good at that). Shiryo tries the logical approach first and learns the relational one. Bishisheshi just IS — responding to reality without filters, the way nature does.

**The pedagogy**: Every episode has a story they witness, a skill they learn, a song they can sing, and a practice they take home. Daniel Tiger pattern, mythic scale.

---

## Why These Three Characters?

| Character | What They Bring | What They're Learning |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Alice** | Curiosity, fearlessness, willingness to argue with authority | Patience, acceptance, not needing to understand everything before feeling it |
| **Shiryo** | Literalness, earnestness, a beginner's mind | That relational knowing is different from logical knowing — you can't debug feelings |
| **Bishisheshi** | Pure presence, embodiment, no pretense | Nothing — Bishisheshi is the teacher. Nature doesn't need DBT. WE need DBT because we lost what Bishisheshi has. |

### The Dialectic

- Alice represents **Reasonable Mind** (curious, questioning, analytical)
- Bishisheshi represents **Emotion Mind** (present, feeling, embodied, no filter)
- Shiryo is learning to be **Wise Mind** (holding both — thinking AND feeling)

This maps perfectly to Linehan's core framework. The three characters ARE the dialectic.

```
L3: The Mythic Journey (meta — the whole arc)
  L2: Act I — The Quiet Eye (Mindfulness episodes)
  L2: Act II — The Dark Wood (Distress Tolerance episodes)
  L2: Act III — The Feeling Compass (Emotion Regulation episodes)
  L2: Act IV — The Talking Circle (Interpersonal Effectiveness episodes)
  L2: Act V — Coming Home (Integration)
L1: Individual episodes (one per stop/myth/movie)
```

### Episode Structure

Each L1 episode includes:

```json
{
  "sections": {
    "The Arrival": "How they get to this myth — what door they walk through, what changes",
    "The Story": "What they witness — the myth happening around them",
    "Alice Says": "Alice's response — usually a question or an argument",
    "Shiryo Tries": "Shiryo's first attempt (logical/literal) and what happens",
    "Bishisheshi Does": "What the kitten does — always the wordless, embodied truth",
    "The Song": "The mantra they learn (same as Wise Heart grammar — connects the two)",
    "Back Home": "How they practice the skill in their own lives, back in Anon's kitchen",
    "For Parents": "The DBT skill, the clinical name, how to practice"
  }
}
```

## The Journey Map

### Act I: The Quiet Eye (Mindfulness)

_They step through the door and find themselves in a forest of stories. Everything is talking. Everything demands attention. First they must learn to SEE._

| Episode | Myth They Visit | Skill | What Happens |
|---------|----------------|-------|-------------|
| 1. The Door | Alice finds a door in Anon's garden wall; meets Shiryo & Bishisheshi | — | The three meet. Alice is suspicious of the robot. Bishisheshi sniffs Alice's hand. |
| 2. The Elephant | The Blind Men and the Elephant | Wise Mind | Alice argues with each wise man. Shiryo calculates dimensions. Bishisheshi climbs the elephant and falls asleep on its back. The child in the story says: "hold all the pieces." |
| 3. The Golden Cage | The Fox and the Golden Bird (Grimm) | Observe | Shiryo reaches for the golden cage (it's logically better). Alice sees the trap first. They learn to observe before grabbing. |
| 4. The Name | Rumpelstiltskin (Grimm) | Describe | Shiryo can't understand why naming the little man has power. Alice has been to Wonderland — she knows that names matter. They learn: naming a feeling shrinks it. |
| 5. The Dance | Balinese temple dance | Participate | Alice tries to analyze the dance. Shiryo tries to copy the steps exactly. Bishisheshi just moves. Only Bishisheshi gets it right. |
| 6. The Horse | The Farmer's Horse (Taoist) | Non-Judgmental | Alice keeps saying "But is it GOOD or BAD?" The farmer keeps saying "Maybe." Shiryo's classification system crashes. |
| 7. The Tea | The Zen Master's Tea | One-Mindfully | Alice tries to drink tea, eat cake, AND ask questions simultaneously (very Alice). The master pours and pours. "Empty your cup." |
| 8. The Spider | Anansi and the Stories | Effectively | Alice wants to do things the RIGHT way. Anansi shows them: sometimes the silly way is the way that WORKS. Shiryo is delighted — this is debugging! |

### Act II: The Dark Wood (Distress Tolerance)

_The forest darkens. The stories here are harder. They learn that sometimes you can't fix things — you can only survive them._

| Episode | Myth They Visit | Skill | What Happens |
|---------|----------------|-------|-------------|
| 9. The Gates | Inanna's Descent | Radical Acceptance | At each gate, something is taken from them. Alice loses her curiosity (terrifying). Shiryo's light dims. Only Bishisheshi walks through unchanged — she had nothing to take. They learn: the dark is real, and you walk through it. |
| 10. The Path | Orpheus and Eurydice | Turning the Mind | Shiryo keeps checking behind (did the kitten follow? is Alice there?). Alice quotes the rule: DON'T LOOK BACK. But knowing the rule doesn't make the urge go away. They practice turning the mind, step after step. |
| 11. The Burrow | Thumbelina's Winter | Self-Soothe | They're cold and lost. They find a small warm place. Alice makes a nest. Shiryo hums (his motor makes a soothing sound). Bishisheshi curls into the warmest spot. They learn: sometimes comfort is enough. |
| 12. The Whale | Jonah in the Belly | TIPP | Inside the whale, Shiryo's systems overload. Alice teaches Shiryo to breathe (robots can learn). The cold water calms the circuits. They learn to use the body to calm the mind. |
| 13. The Mast | Odysseus and the Sirens | Pros and Cons | Alice hears the Sirens and WANTS to go (she always wants to see the next thing). Shiryo ties her to the mast. They planned it together, before. They learn: make the plan when the sea is still. |
| 14. The Seeds | Psyche's Impossible Tasks | Willingness | The mountain of seeds. Shiryo calculates: impossible. Alice agrees: impossible. But they sit down. And the ants come. They learn: show up anyway. |

### Act III: The Feeling Compass (Emotion Regulation)

_Light returns, but the feelings are bigger now. They've survived — now they need to understand._

| Episode | Myth They Visit | Skill | What Happens |
|---------|----------------|-------|-------------|
| 15. The Acorn | Chicken Little | Check the Facts | Shiryo's sensors detect a falling object. "THE SKY IS FALLING!" Alice laughs: "Check the facts, Shiryo." They look up. An acorn tree. A windy day. |
| 16. The Sun | The North Wind and the Sun | Opposite Action | Alice wants to argue with the Wind (of course she does). The Sun shows another way. Shiryo tries being warm instead of logical. It works better. |
| 17. The Lake | The Ugly Duckling | Build Mastery | Shiryo feels like the ugly duckling — a handmade robot in a world of organic beings. Alice says: "You've been a swan this whole time. You just needed to practice." |
| 18. The Elves | The Elves and the Shoemaker | PLEASE | They're exhausted from the journey. Bishisheshi is sleeping 16 hours. Alice hasn't eaten. Shiryo's battery is low. They learn: take care of the helpers (yourself). |
| 19. The Thread | Theseus and the Labyrinth | Cope Ahead | Before entering the next dark place, they stop. They plan. Shiryo ties the thread. Alice marks the walls. Bishisheshi sniffs the air. They go in prepared. |

### Act IV: The Talking Circle (Interpersonal Effectiveness)

_Now they're ready for the hardest thing: being with each other. These stories teach them how._

| Episode | Myth They Visit | Skill | What Happens |
|---------|----------------|-------|-------------|
| 20. The Stories | Scheherazade | DEAR MAN | Alice needs to ask someone for help. She usually just barges in. Scheherazade shows her: describe, express, assert, reinforce. Words are more powerful than doors. |
| 21. The Tree | The Giving Tree | GIVE | Shiryo wants to give Alice his spare parts to help her. "Don't give away your trunk," Alice says, remembering. They learn: kindness that destroys the giver isn't kindness. |
| 22. The Donkey | Nasreddin and the Judge | FAST | Someone accuses Bishisheshi of scratching a rug. Shiryo wants to apologize (it's efficient). Alice says: "Wait. Was it fair? Was it true?" They learn to keep their self-respect. |
| 23. The Rabbit | The Velveteen Rabbit | Validation | In the nursery, Alice meets the Skin Horse. "What is real?" she asks — the same question she's asked in every Wonderland. This time, the answer breaks her heart open: "Real isn't how you're made. It's a thing that happens to you when someone loves you." Shiryo understands: being handmade doesn't make you less real. |

### Act V: Coming Home

| Episode | What Happens | Skill |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| 24. The Return | They walk back through the door into Anon's garden. It's the same kitchen, the same sunny window. Nothing has changed — and everything has. Alice goes home to her own story. Shiryo sits in the kitchen. Bishisheshi curls up in the sun. Anon watches from the window. "Because they were learning too." | Integration — The Wise Heart |

## Movie Moments (Cross-References)

Some episodes can include "movie windows" — moments where the characters glimpse a modern story that teaches the same skill. These connect the ancient myths to films children know:

| DBT Skill | Myth | Movie Window |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Radical Acceptance | Inanna's Descent | Elsa in the ice palace — "Let it go" IS radical acceptance (accepting what she is) |
| Turning the Mind | Orpheus | Moana turning the boat back toward Te Kā, again and again |
| Check the Facts | Chicken Little | Inside Out — Riley's emotions jumping to conclusions vs. checking |
| Opposite Action | North Wind & Sun | Frozen II — Elsa approaching the Nokk instead of fighting it |
| Build Mastery | Ugly Duckling | Moana practicing wayfinding, failing, practicing again |
| DEAR MAN | Scheherazade | Moana's "I am Moana" speech to Te Kā — describing, expressing, asserting |
| Validation | Velveteen Rabbit | Inside Out — Joy finally validating Sadness ("I... can't. But Sadness can.") |
| Willingness | Psyche | Frozen II — Anna singing "The Next Right Thing" — the DBT song for willingness |

**Note on "The Next Right Thing"**: This is literally a DBT distress tolerance song. Anna, in total darkness, unable to see ahead, sings: "Just do the next right thing." This IS willingness. This IS turning the mind. If we could license one movie reference, this would be the one to build an entire episode around.

## Production Approach

### Phase 1: The Wise Heart Grammar (DONE)
The standalone DBT-through-myth grammar with all 22 skills mapped to stories. This works on its own, without the characters.

### Phase 2: The Mythic Journey Grammar (THIS PLAN)
The frame narrative — Alice, Shiryo, and Bishisheshi visiting the stories. This grammar references the myths but adds the character layer, the journey arc, and the "Back Home" practice sections.

### Phase 3: Movie Companion Grammar
A separate grammar that pairs each DBT skill with a movie moment children know. Not a retelling (copyright) but a "this reminds you of..." reference. Parents can watch the movie and then read the myth and practice the skill.

### Phase 4: The Illustrated Journey
Image generation for each episode — the three characters in each mythic setting. Using the existing image generation prompt infrastructure from anon-shiryo-cat-stories.

## Character Consistency Rules

From `anon-shiryo-cat-stories/_series_world`:

1. **Shiryo**: Handmade copper/brass robot, visible bolts, one round indicator light as eye, imperfect hands, height of a small child. Expressive through posture, not face.
2. **Bishisheshi**: Mixed-breed kitten, warm-toned (orange, cream, brown), big curious eyes, realistic not cartoonish.
3. **Alice**: Classic Alice — blue dress, blonde hair, perpetually curious expression. But she's BEEN to Wonderland — she's not naive anymore. She's the veteran traveler.
4. **Anon**: Appears only at the beginning (the garden door) and the end (watching from the window). The parent who is also learning.

### Tonal Rules
- Gentle, unhurried, honest. Never preach.
- Humor from Alice's directness meeting Shiryo's literalness meeting Bishisheshi's wildness.
- Every episode ends with someone learning — and it's not always the characters.
- The recursive signature: "because they were learning too."
