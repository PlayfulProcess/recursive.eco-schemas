**Status:** Future session

## The Need

The tattva deck now has oracle usage philosophy in its description, but this applies to ALL oracle grammars on recursive.eco (I Ching, tarot, tattvas, future decks). It should live on a site-level page, not repeated per grammar.

## Key Points (from Wallis feedback)

1. **"The card is a mirror, not a map."** You are not asking what will happen. You are asking which aspect of your own consciousness is presenting itself for recognition.

2. **The near enemy is conceptual bypassing.** Drawing a card and thinking "my problem is an illusion" instead of looking at what is actually happening. The oracle is not for getting answers about your situation — it is for getting in contact with your actual experience.

3. **The Practice field is the real work.** The practices are dharaṇā-style inquiries (contemplative techniques). The oracle mechanism is a randomizer for selecting which one to do today — structurally identical to how the VBT's 112 techniques are used in practice.

4. **Connect to the upāya vocabulary:**
   - Casting and doing the Practice = āṇavopāya (technique-based)
   - Letting a single insight dissolve something = śāktopāya (contemplative)
   - The whole structure falling away in recognition = śāmbhavopāya (grace)

5. **The tradition's own precedent:** The VBT says master ONE technique. Any one. The tradition did not expect sequential completion. This is functionally identical to drawing a card.

6. **Pratibhā** — innate intuitive insight. The random selection isn't the oracle's wisdom. Your response to it is.
