# Sword and Grail: King Arthur for Bedtime — Design Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Grammar slug:** `king-arthur-kids`
**Grammar type:** `custom`
**Approach:** From memory, rhyming verse, esoteric/shamanic lens
**Format:** Same as biblical-stories-kids: 3 stanzas, AABB/ABAB, ALL CAPS keywords

---

## Vision

King Arthur retold as shamanic mythology for children. Merlin is the shaman — the last Druid, the bridge between the old nature-religion and the incoming Christian mystery. The Grail isn't a cup — it's the vessel of divine consciousness (same as the Cosmic Christ in the biblical grammar). Avalon is the Otherworld — not heaven, not death, but the dimension that exists alongside ours. The Round Table is a mandala — a circle where no one sits higher than anyone else.

Esoteric sources:
- **Rudolf Steiner** — The Grail as etheric vessel, Parsifal as the one who asks the right question
- **Joseph Campbell** — The hero with a thousand faces, the Grail quest as individuation
- **Jessie Weston** — From Ritual to Romance (the Grail as pre-Christian fertility ritual)
- **Geoffrey of Monmouth / Malory / Chrétien de Troyes** — The source texts
- **Celtic mythology** — The Morrigan, the Otherworld, Brigid, the Tuatha Dé Danann

## Illustration Sources

### Primary: Aubrey Beardsley — Le Morte d'Arthur (1893-94)
- **~350 illustrations**: Black and white Art Nouveau, stunning decorative borders
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons, all public domain
- **Style:** Bold black-and-white, sinuous lines, erotic-mystical atmosphere
- **Perfect for:** The dark, enchanted tone of this grammar

### Secondary: Howard Pyle — The Story of King Arthur and His Knights (1903)
- **~75 illustrations**: Golden Age illustration, rich detail
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons, public domain
- **Style:** Detailed pen-and-ink with heroic compositions
- **Best for:** The action stories, the quests, the battles

### Tertiary: Walter Crane — King Arthur's Knights (1911)
- **~16 color plates**: Arts & Crafts movement
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons, public domain
- **Style:** Decorative, flat color, tapestry-like
- **Best for:** L2 and L3 cover images

### Supplementary: Edward Burne-Jones
- Pre-Raphaelite paintings of Arthurian subjects
- **The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon** (1881-1898) — perfect for `return-avalon`
- **The Beguiling of Merlin** — perfect for Merlin stories
- Grail tapestry series at Birmingham Museum
