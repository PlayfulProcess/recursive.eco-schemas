# Plan: Myths Through Many Eyes — A Comparative Mythology Grammar

## Core Concept

**L1 = The myths themselves.** Brief, faithful retellings of ~25 myths in their original cultural voice — not interpreted, just told. Each myth card names its culture, its source text, and its core images.

**L2 = The interpreters as emergence.** Jung, Campbell, Otto Rank, Frazer, the alchemists — each is an L2 card that references (via `composite_of`) the specific myths they quoted or analyzed. These cards show HOW each thinker read the myths, what patterns they saw, which myths were their touchstones.

**L2 also = Thematic threads.** Cross-cultural patterns that emerge when you lay the myths side by side: The Descent, The Dying God, The Sacred Marriage, The Dragon Fight, The Trickster, The Great Mother. Each thread card references the myths that share that pattern.

**L3 = The meta-syntheses.** "The Monomyth" (Campbell's thesis), "The Collective Unconscious" (Jung's thesis), and "The Living Symbol" (the alchemical thesis).

## Why This Structure

The inversion is the point. Most books about myth put the interpreter first and the myths as illustrations. This grammar puts **the myths first** — as living stories, not evidence for someone's theory. Then the interpreters emerge as *readers* of those stories, each seeing different things. The user can draw a myth card and then see: "Jung saw X here, Campbell saw Y, Frazer saw Z, the alchemists saw W."
