## Vision

The Iliad and Odyssey retold as cosmic mythology for children. The gods aren't petty — they're vast cosmic intelligences who can't help meddling because they FEEL everything we feel, amplified to galactic scale. Odysseus isn't just clever — he's the shaman-trickster who talks his way through dimensions. The Trojan War isn't just about Helen — it's about the moment humans stopped letting the gods fight FOR them and started fighting among themselves.

Esoteric sources:
- **Rudolf Steiner** — The Greek gods as real spiritual beings of the third hierarchy
- **Joseph Campbell** — THE source text for the monomyth (especially the Odyssey)
- **Roberto Calasso** — The Marriage of Cadmus and Harmony (gods as forces, not people)
- **Simone Weil** — The Iliad, or the Poem of Force (war as gravity)
- **Celtic/shamanic parallels** — The Otherworld voyage (immram) mirrors the Odyssey

## Illustration Sources

### Primary: John Flaxman — Iliad & Odyssey (1793)
- **~75 line drawings**: Neoclassical, clean, iconic
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons / British Museum, all public domain
- **Style:** Pure line drawing on white — elegant, perfect for children's books
- **Coverage:** Comprehensive for both Iliad and Odyssey

### Secondary: Greek Vase Paintings
- **Hundreds available**: Red-figure and black-figure pottery, 6th-4th century BCE
- **Source:** Metropolitan Museum Open Access, British Museum, Wikimedia
- **Style:** Bold silhouettes, narrative scenes, instantly recognizable
- **Best for:** L2 cluster covers, additional L1 variety

### Tertiary: William Russell Flint — Odyssey (1924)
- **~20 color plates**: Edwardian watercolors, lush and atmospheric
- **Source:** Some on Wikimedia, others may need sourcing
- **Style:** Full color, romantic, dreamlike
- **Best for:** L3 meta-arc covers, the Voyage stories

### Supplementary: Frederic Leighton, John William Waterhouse
- Pre-Raphaelite/Victorian paintings of Homeric subjects
- Waterhouse's **Circe**, **Penelope and the Suitors**, **Odysseus and the Sirens**
- Leighton's **Helen of Troy**, **The Return of Persephone**
