# The Composting Shelf

> "What you refuse to look at controls you." — Jung's shadow principle

## Philosophy

The composting shelf contains texts that represent the intellectual architecture of colonialism, white supremacy, and racial hierarchy — **not for endorsement, but for decomposition**. You cannot understand decolonial thinkers (Fanon, Césaire, Achebe, Andreotti) without encountering what they were responding to. A wisdom library that pretends these ideas don't exist is incomplete.

The metaphor is deliberate: composting takes toxic material and breaks it down so it can feed new growth. Seeds → compost → grammars → emergence.

## Grammar Structure for Composting Texts

Every grammar on this shelf should follow this pattern:

- **L1 items**: The raw primary text (chapters, passages, arguments) — presented without editorializing
- **L2 emergence**: Critical context — who was harmed, real-world impact, which thinkers responded, how these ideas mutated and persisted
- **Sections**: Each L1 item should include:
  - `"Text"` — the original passage
  - `"Critical Context"` — historical impact, who was targeted, what this enabled
  - `"Responses"` — key decolonial/anti-racist thinkers who directly addressed this idea
  - `"Reflection"` — questions for the reader's own shadow work
- **Shelf**: `["composting"]`
- **Worldview**: `"supremacist"`, `"colonial"`, or `"racial-science"` (name it clearly)

## Pairing Strategy

The most powerful grammars will PAIR a colonial text with its response:

1. **Conrad ↔ Achebe**: Heart of Darkness + Achebe's critique (can reference Achebe in L2 critical context even if we can't include the full text)
2. **Grant/Stoddard ↔ Du Bois**: American eugenics + Black intellectual response
3. **Kipling ↔ Fanon**: Imperial duty + colonial liberation (reference Fanon in L2)
4. **Bailey/Blavatsky ↔ ???**: Spiritual racism needs its decomposers — Andreotti's work on "hospicing modernity" is relevant but under copyright
5. **Las Casas** is unique — a colonizer who became a witness against colonialism
6. **Gobineau ↔ everyone**: His race science influenced nearly every text on this list

## Build Priority

1. **Heart of Darkness** — most widely taught, Achebe's response is well-known
2. **Souls of Black Folk** — already a seed, Du Bois is the essential decomposer
3. **Gobineau** — foundational, everything traces back here
4. **Douglass Narrative** — already a seed, the primary counter-document
5. **Stoddard + Grant** — the American eugenics pair
6. **Las Casas** — earliest colonial witness text (1552!)
7. **Bailey** — the esoteric vector (needs sourcing outside Gutenberg)
