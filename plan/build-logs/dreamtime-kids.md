## Cultural Sensitivity Approach

1. **Only publicly shared stories**: Used only stories that Aboriginal storytellers and elders have themselves published or performed for broad audiences (Rainbow Serpent, Tiddalick, kookaburra, etc.)

2. **Credited nations where possible**: Each Whisper section names the Aboriginal nation associated with the version being told.

3. **Acknowledged protocols**: Whisper sections note that Aboriginal stories operate within cultural protocols — some are public, some are restricted by age, gender, or initiation level.

4. **Pointed to Aboriginal voices**: Rather than claiming authority, each Whisper section points toward Aboriginal-authored sources.

5. **Inclusion is not appropriation**: The series plan notes that excluding the world's oldest continuous spiritual tradition while including every Indo-European mythology would itself be a colonial act. The third way: share what's been shared, credit who shared it.

## Learnings

1. **The Dreaming is NOT "the past"**: This is the hardest concept for Western minds. The Dreaming is an eternal present — the land IS the Dreaming, right now. Songlines are not historical records; they are living navigational-spiritual practices.

2. **Country as a relative**: In Aboriginal thought, Country is not a resource — it's a RELATIVE. You have obligations to Country the way you have obligations to family. This is the most radical animism in the series.

3. **65,000 years of continuity**: These stories are 10× older than anything else in the series. The time-depth gives every other tradition context.

4. **The Law arc is unique**: No other grammar in the series has an arc devoted to LIVING CORRECTLY. The Dreaming doesn't just tell you what happened — it tells you how to live. This is the grammar's most practical gift.
