# Build Log: The Story Tree — Children's Stories for Every Age

## Session 1 (2026-03-06)

### What happened
- Explored all 48 existing grammars that could cross-link to this new grammar
- Designed a comprehensive plan with 33 L1 stories, 10 L2 groups, 5 L3 traditions = 48 items total
- Wrote detailed plan to `plan/children-stories-grammar-plan.md`
- **Failed to execute**: Stalled after writing the plan. The scope (165 content sections) was too large to tackle without breaking it into phases.

### Lesson learned
- 48 items with 5 rich sections each (preschool telling, classic telling, cultural roots, modern reflection, for parents) = ~165 substantial content sections
- This is well beyond the ~80-item guideline in CLAUDE.md for single-session from-memory grammars
- Should have immediately started the skeleton after the plan instead of pausing
- The right approach: skeleton first (commit checkpoint), then fill content group-by-group across multiple edit passes

### Revised approach
- Build skeleton with all structure + placeholder sections (Phase 1)
- Fill content systematically group by group (Phase 2+)
- Commit after each completed group so progress is never lost
