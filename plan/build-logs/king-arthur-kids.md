## Writing Approach

- Same format as biblical-stories-kids: 3 stanzas, AABB/ABAB, ALL CAPS keywords
- Shamanic lens throughout: Merlin as shaman, Grail as etheric vessel, Avalon as Otherworld
- Campbell monomyth mapped for every story
- Pop culture parallels (Star Wars/LOTR/Harry Potter) for instant kid recognition
- Jessie Weston's "From Ritual to Romance" as key esoteric source for Grail material

## Learnings

1. **Arthurian material is darker**: Compared to biblical stories, the Arthur cycle has more moral ambiguity — Lancelot's affair, Mordred's conception, the suitors' slaughter. The rhyming verse format handles this well by keeping the tone light while the Whisper sections carry the weight.

2. **The Grail arc is the hardest**: Writing about the Grail for kids while maintaining the esoteric depth requires careful layering. The L1 stories tell the adventure; the L2 Thread sections carry the Steiner/Weston material; the L3 Pattern sections synthesize.

3. **18 L2 clusters vs 29 for biblical**: The Arthur material groups more naturally into 3 clusters per arc (beginnings/middle/end), whereas the biblical material had more thematic crosscutting. Both approaches work.
