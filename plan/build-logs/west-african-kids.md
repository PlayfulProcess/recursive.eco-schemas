## Learnings

1. **Diaspora dimension is essential**: This grammar is unique in the series — it covers not just the original mythology but its SURVIVAL and TRANSFORMATION across the Atlantic. The Crossing and Return arcs make this grammar about resilience and continuity.

2. **Orisha traditions are living religions**: Careful to note that Santería, Candomblé, and Vodou are active faith traditions, not historical mythology. Whisper sections note this.

3. **Call-and-response structure**: The only tradition that builds audience participation INTO the storytelling form. Reflected in the Wonder sections.

4. **Anansi as meta-narrative**: The spider who owns ALL stories is the most meta character in world mythology — he's the patron saint of storytellers. Perfect link to the One Story meta-grammar.
