## Learnings

1. **The Hero Twins are unique**: They defeat death through TRICKS and GAMES, not weapons or divine power. This makes the Popol Vuh the most creative hero narrative in world mythology.

2. **Xibalba's dark humor**: The lords of death are named things like "Pus Master" and "Bone Staff." The underworld is simultaneously terrifying and absurd. This tonal range is hard in rhyming verse but rewarding.

3. **Corn as consciousness**: Humans are literally MADE of corn in Maya cosmology. "You are what you eat" is not metaphor — it's creation theology. This connects to modern food sovereignty movements.

4. **Colonial survival document**: The Popol Vuh was written down under Spanish colonial pressure — it's both a sacred text and an act of cultural preservation. The Whisper sections honor this context.

5. **Failed creations are the best part**: The mud people and wood people (failed humans) are hilarious and profound. Creation requires FAILURE first.
